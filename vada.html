Vada Recipe

Home Back to Breakfast
Vada is a crispy, savory South Indian snack made from lentil flour and spices. It's often enjoyed with coconut chutney or sambar. This recipe will guide you through making perfect, golden-brown vadas with a crunchy exterior and soft interior.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Urad Dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 cup Finely Chopped Onions
  • 2 Green Chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Ginger, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Coriander Leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Curry Leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Soak urad dal in water for 4-5 hours or overnight. Drain and grind to a smooth batter, adding a little water as needed.
  2. Transfer the batter to a bowl and mix in the chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, coriander leaves, curry leaves, cumin seeds, black pepper, and salt.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai. Wet your hands or use a spoon to shape small portions of the batter into round discs with a hole in the center.
  4. Carefully slide the vadas into the hot oil and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  5. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

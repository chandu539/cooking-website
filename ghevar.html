Ghevar Recipe

Home Back to Desserts
Ghevar is a traditional Rajasthani sweet, particularly popular during festivals. This round, honeycomb-like dessert is made from a batter of flour, milk, and ghee, fried until crispy, and then soaked in sugar syrup for a delectable treat.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup All-Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 cup Cornflour
  • 1/2 cup Milk
  • 1/2 cup Ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Powder
  • 1 cup Sugar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1/4 tsp Cardamom Powder
  • Chopped Nuts for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornflour, and baking powder. Gradually add milk and whisk to make a smooth batter.
  2. Heat ghee in a deep frying pan. To test if the ghee is hot enough, drop a small amount of batter into the ghee; it should float and sizzle.
  3. Pour a small amount of batter into the hot ghee. The batter should spread out and form a honeycomb-like structure. Fry until golden brown, then remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. Prepare the sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water together until slightly thickened. Add cardamom powder and mix well.
  5. Soak the fried ghevars in the warm sugar syrup for a few minutes, then remove and let them cool. Garnish with chopped nuts if desired.

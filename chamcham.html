Chamcham Recipe

Home Back to Desserts
Chamcham is a traditional Bengali sweet made from paneer or chenna. These soft, cylindrical sweets are soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup and garnished with coconut, making them a popular choice for celebrations and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter Milk
  • 2 tbsp Lemon Juice or Vinegar
  • 1 cup Sugar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1/2 tsp Cardamom Powder
  • 2 tbsp Grated Coconut (for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp Rose Water (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Boil the milk in a pan, then add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it. Once curdled, strain the curds through a muslin cloth to remove the whey. Rinse the curds under cold water to stop further cooking.
  2. Press the curds to remove excess water and then knead them into a smooth dough. Shape the dough into small cylindrical pieces.
  3. In another pan, prepare the sugar syrup by combining sugar and water. Bring it to a boil, then add cardamom powder and rose water if using.
  4. Gently drop the shaped pieces into the simmering syrup. Cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the chamchams become soft and have absorbed the syrup.
  5. Remove from heat and let the chamchams cool in the syrup. Garnish with grated coconut before serving.

Rasagulla Recipe

Home Back to Desserts
Rasagulla, also known as Rasgulla, is a popular Bengali sweet made from soft, spongy balls of chenna soaked in a light sugar syrup. It is enjoyed for its delicate texture and sweet flavor, making it a favorite for special occasions and festivals.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter Milk
  • 2 tbsp Lemon Juice or Vinegar
  • 1 cup Sugar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1/2 tsp Cardamom Powder
  • 1 tsp Rose Water (optional)
  • 1 tsp Chopped Pistachios (for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Boil the milk in a pan, then add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it. Strain the curds through a muslin cloth to remove the whey. Rinse the curds under cold water.
  2. Press the curds to remove excess water, then knead them into a smooth dough. Shape the dough into small round balls.
  3. In another pan, prepare the sugar syrup by combining sugar and water. Bring it to a boil and then add cardamom powder and rose water if using.
  4. Gently drop the shaped balls into the simmering syrup. Cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rasagullas are soft and have absorbed the syrup.
  5. Remove from heat and let the rasagullas cool in the syrup. Garnish with chopped pistachios before serving.

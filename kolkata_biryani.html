Kolkata Biryani Recipe

Home Back to Biryani
Kolkata Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic dish with a unique blend of spices, potatoes, and tender meat. Unlike other biryanis, the Kolkata version is known for its subtle flavors and the use of boiled eggs and potatoes, which add a rich texture to the dish. Perfect for special occasions, this biryani is a must-try for all food lovers.

Ingredients

  • 500g Mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups Basmati Rice
  • 2 large Potatoes, peeled and halved
  • 2 large Onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 large Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 4 Green Chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup Mint Leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Coriander Leaves, chopped
  • Whole Spices (Cinnamon, Cloves, Cardamom, Bay Leaf)
  • 2 tbsp Biryani Masala
  • 1 tbsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp Ghee
  • Saffron soaked in warm milk
  • 4 Boiled Eggs

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate the mutton with yogurt, ginger garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Let it rest for 1 hour.
  2. Fry the potatoes in hot oil until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. In a large pot, heat ghee and add the whole spices. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Then, add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add green chilies, mint, and coriander leaves. Mix well.
  6. Add the marinated mutton and cook until tender.
  7. In another pot, boil water, add salt, and cook the basmati rice until it's 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  8. Layer the rice over the mutton mixture. Place the fried potatoes and boiled eggs on top. Drizzle saffron milk and ghee over the top.
  9. Cover with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  10. Once done, fluff the rice gently with a fork. Serve hot with raita and salad.

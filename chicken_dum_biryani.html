Chicken Dhum Biryani Recipe

Home Back to Biryani
Chicken Dhum Biryani is a traditional Indian dish known for its rich flavors and enticing aroma. This recipe involves marinating chicken in a blend of spices and yogurt, then cooking it with partially cooked basmati rice. The dish is then slow-cooked ('dum') to allow the flavors to fully develop. It’s a favorite at celebrations and a must-try for anyone who loves Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Chicken, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 3 cups Basmati Rice
  • 2 large Onions, sliced
  • 2 large Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 4 Green Chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup Mint Leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Coriander Leaves, chopped
  • Whole Spices (Cinnamon, Cloves, Cardamom, Bay Leaf)
  • 2 tbsp Biryani Masala
  • 1 tbsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • Few strands of Saffron soaked in warm milk
  • 4 tbsp Ghee
  • Salt to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, ginger garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Let it rest for at least 1 hour.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee and add the whole spices. Once fragrant, add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add tomatoes and green chilies, and cook until the tomatoes turn soft and mushy.
  4. Add the marinated chicken and cook on medium-high heat until the chicken is half-cooked.
  5. Add biryani masala, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. In another pot, boil water with salt and cook the basmati rice until it's 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  7. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Drizzle saffron milk and additional ghee over the rice.
  8. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  9. Once done, gently fluff the rice with a fork. Serve hot with raita or salad.

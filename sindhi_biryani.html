Sindhi Biryani Recipe

Home Back to Biryani
Sindhi Biryani is a spicy and flavorful biryani that is popular in the Sindh region of Pakistan. It is known for its rich and aromatic spices, making it a favorite among biryani lovers. The rice is cooked with succulent pieces of meat and a variety of spices, resulting in a dish that is bursting with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 500g Mutton or Chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups Basmati Rice
  • 2 large Onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 large Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 4 Green Chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup Mint Leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Coriander Leaves, chopped
  • Whole Spices (Cinnamon, Cloves, Cardamom, Bay Leaf)
  • 2 tbsp Biryani Masala
  • 1 tbsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp Ghee
  • Saffron soaked in warm milk

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate the meat with yogurt, ginger garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Let it rest for 1 hour.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee and add the whole spices. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Then, add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Add green chilies, mint, and coriander leaves. Mix well.
  5. Add the marinated meat and cook on low heat until the meat is tender.
  6. In another pot, boil water, add salt, and cook the basmati rice until it's 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  7. Layer the rice over the meat mixture. Drizzle saffron milk and ghee over the top.
  8. Cover with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  9. Once done, fluff the rice gently with a fork. Serve hot with raita and salad.
